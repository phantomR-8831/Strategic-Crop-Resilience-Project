Project "Phoenix Bean": Phaseolus Vulgaris as a Strategic Crop for Post-Catastrophic Recovery##
OverviewIn an era of increasing global instability and environmental threats, 
the selection of crops for food security must shift from "maximum yield" to "maximum resilience." 
This project explores the unique genetic and physiological potential of Phaseolus vulgaris (Common Bean) as a primary survival crop in extreme scenarios, 
including high-radiation environments.## Strategic Characteristics (The "Survival" Advantage)Nutritional 
Autonomy: High protein content (20-25%) provides a vital meat substitute in broken supply chain conditions.
Soil Regeneration: Natural nitrogen fixation (via symbiotic Rhizobia) restores soil fertility without the need for synthetic fertilizers.
Low Input Requirement: Minimal agrochemical and energy demands compared to corn or wheat.Waste-Free Utility:Seeds: 
Human nutrition.Pods (Pericarpium Phaseoli): Used in pharmaceutical production (hypoglycemic properties, metabolic support).
Biomass: Fodder or green manure.##
Radiation Resilience & RadiobiologyOne of the most critical findings of this research is the behavior of the bean genome under ionizing stress (up to 5600 R):
Selective Radio-resistance: Identification of phenotypes capable of maintaining germination and fertility after high-dose exposure.
Low Radionuclide Accumulation: Unlike many other crops, certain bean varieties exhibit a natural barrier against accumulating heavy radionuclides in the edible seeds.
Detoxification Potential: High concentration of antioxidants and specific fibers in beans aids in the elimination of radionuclides from the human body.##
